Music and Podcasts

Music has a profound effect on our emotions and mental state. It can help reduce stress, improve focus, and elevate mood. Different genres of music can evoke various emotions, such as calmness, happiness, or excitement. By understanding the impact of music on the brain, individuals can use it as a tool to manage emotions, enhance mental clarity, and foster relaxation.

Music therapy is widely used to treat conditions like depression and anxiety, offering an expressive outlet for those struggling with these mental health challenges. Moreover, creating personalized playlists for different moods can be a powerful way to harness the therapeutic effects of music.

When to Use Music to Help

Music is said to enhance intelligence and focus, improve mental health, and boost the immune system as well as self-esteem and confidence. It can be used to relax, to boost and lift our mood, or to improve concentration. Music can also be used to aid in insomnia, helping to encourage and induce a deeper sleep. Many people have expressed how music is a form of ‘escape’ and can quickly and effectively transport them to a better time, place, or memory. Here are six benefits that music therapy can have on your mental health:

  1. Improve Focus on Work or Study: There have been numerous studies proving that listening to classical music helps the mind to focus. Music with a tempo of 60 bpm (beats per minute) increases the brain’s ability to process information. There have also been studies suggesting that EDM (electronic dance music) helps sharpen focus while studying.
  2. As a Form of Expression: Music is a wonderful way of expressing emotion and creativity without having to say anything. Whether it’s playing a playlist of your favorite songs with friends or strumming your guitar, music is the perfect non-verbal way of expressing emotions that are too complex to talk about.
  3. To Lift the Mood: Playing uplifting songs has a profound effect on the brain, stimulating it to increase feel-good hormones which boost our mood.
  4. To Boost Confidence: Subliminal and ambient music such as binaural beat music is proven to assist in anxiety and low-confidence. Using alpha waves from 8-14 Hz help the mind get into a more sharpened and confident state of mind. Listening to binaural beat and isochronic tone music is ideal when you’re feeling nervous or fearful. It is believed that the brain syncs to the new frequency introduced by the binaural beat, altering your ‘brain state’ to a happier, more confident sense of being.
  5. To Relax: Meditative music has a cathartic effect on us, improving our mood and inducing relaxation of both the body and mind. Research has shown the calming sounds of piano, nature, and string instruments are very effective in helping you to relax.
  6. To Express Anger or Negative Emotion: Music is an incredible vehicle to help us process negative emotion. Listening to ‘angry’ music can be therapeutic when we’re dealing with stress and anger.

Types of Music Therapy

There are different types of music therapy to utilize, depending on specific cases and treatment strategies. Anyone can benefit from music therapy, including those suffering from depression, anxiety, substance abuse, autism, neurological diseases, personality disorders, insomnia, and dementia. Because music evokes positive emotions and stimulates reward centers in the brain, music therapy is used to alleviate patients through:

Singing or chanting

Playing musical instruments

Dancing to music

Relaxing and meditating to music

Music therapists have numerous techniques to help patients heal. Music therapy can be administered individually or in group therapy sessions, which also help to create a sense of community between clients, forming connections and a supportive hub encouraging self-expression and exploration.

Affect of Music on Mental Health

Our brain can be divided into 4 parts - frontal lobe, temporal lobe, occipital lobe, and parietal lobe. When we listen to music, all four parts react. Though all of them work on different aspects, they all respond. When music enters our brain, it is split into pitch and volume. After that comes the feeling of emotions.

The different effects music can have on your brain depend upon the genre we prefer to listen to.

  • Soothing music like classical music helps us distress and has healing powers.
  • Upbeat music releases endorphins which make us happy and increases our immune system.
  • Pop music boosts energy and helps us exercise better.
  • Rhythmic drumming helps in meditation.
  • Metal is good for our sense of self and gives a strong sense of identity.
  • Rap music can help fight against depression.
  • Country music evokes feelings of internal joy.
  • Classical music helps to manage stress.

What Are Podcasts?

Podcasts are a type of audio programming that you can listen to on your computer, phone, or other smart devices. Some of the most popular streaming platforms to access podcasts include Spotify, Apple, iHeartRadio, Pandora, and TuneIn. They are usually available for free, and many podcasts are produced by independent creators. This means there is a wide range of podcasts available on a variety of topics.

Podcasts have become popular in recent years, as they offer an easy and accessible way to listen to audio content. You can listen to podcasts while you're doing other things, like commuting, cooking, or working out. This makes them a convenient way to fit learning and entertainment into your life.

Podcasts can also be a great way to learn about mental health. There are ample podcasts that offer educational information on topics like anxiety, depression, and self-care. This can help to increase understanding and reduce stigma. In a society that can be quick to judge and stigmatize mental illness, podcasts can provide a space for open and honest discussion. Learning about mental health can also empower people to take care of their mental health, and support others.

Mental Health Benefits of Listening to Podcasts

  • Reduce stress and anxiety: Podcasts can be a great way to relax and de-stress. When you're feeling overwhelmed, taking a break to listen to a podcast can help to center and calm you.
  • Provide emotional support: Listening to podcasts can feel like having a conversation with a friend. Many podcasts offer listeners a sense of connection and community. This can be especially helpful if you're feeling isolated or alone.
  • Gain new perspectives: Podcasts can offer a new perspective on life. They can help you to learn about other people's experiences and to see the world in a different way.
  • Increase self-awareness: Podcasts can help you to better understand yourself, your thoughts, and your emotions. This self-awareness is essential for managing mental health.
  • Promote positive mental health: By increasing understanding and reducing stigma, podcasts can help to promote positive mental health. This can empower people to take care of their mental health and support others.
